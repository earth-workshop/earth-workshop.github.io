üåç Embodied AI and Robotics for HealTHcare (EARTH)

The field of medical intervention has consistently led the integration of cutting-edge technologies to improve patient healthcare. The MICCAI community has been pivotal in fostering innovations in this domain, with a special focus on the exploration and application of artificial intelligence (AI) and robotics. The proposed workshop, Embodied AI and Robotics for HealTHcare (EARTH), aims to advance the discourse on surgical intervention by focusing on embodied AI, including the latest advancements in Large Language Models (LLMs), and robotics. This integration is crucial for enhancing the precision, adaptability, and standardization of surgical procedures.

The EARTH workshop is dedicated to advancing the field of embodied AI and robotics in healthcare by leveraging the latest advancements in general-purpose AI. These general-purpose AI technologies, especially LLMs, demonstrate advanced understanding, reasoning, and planning capabilities, enabling the processing of extensive medical literature, surgical protocols, and robotic functions to support decision-making, outcome prediction, personalized surgical planning, and even surgical robot control. Our goal is to integrate these AI advancements into the development of intelligent surgical robots that are capable of intelligent interactions with the medical environment and human collaborators. Focusing on the interplay between embodied AI and robotics, the EARTH workshop will foster the creation of intuitive, autonomous, and semi-autonomous robotic assistants that learn, adapt, and assist in the complex decision-making required for surgical interventions. Through this workshop, we invite the MICCAI community to engage with this novel and exciting application of embodied AI and robotics technology, which is on the verge of revolutionizing the surgical intervention and setting new standards for patient healthcare.

üìú Call for Papers

The EARTH workshop is proposed to be a multidisciplinary forum where leading researchers will present their latest work in embodied AI and robotics, discuss technological breakthroughs, and examine the confluence of emerging technologies in general-purpose AI and AI-driven control systems. We aspire to explore and understand the transformative impact of these technologies on the future of surgical procedures and healthcare delivery.

  • Embodied AI for Healthcare: Development and application of AI systems that possess a physical presence within the healthcare environment.
  • AI-Driven Robotics: Development of robotic systems that leverage AI for autonomous or semi-autonomous functioning, decision-making support, and procedural learning.
  • Intelligent Surgical Assistants: Development and refinement of robotic systems that can perceive, understand, and interact with their environment to provide real-time assistance during surgical procedures.
  • Surgical Robotics: The design, implementation, and evaluation of robotic systems tailored for assisting and enhancing surgical interventions, especially interpreting medical settings and patient-specific data to make autonomous adjustments.
  • Context-Aware Medical Robotics: Innovations in robotic systems designed for diagnosis, treatment, and rehabilitation in healthcare settings.
  • Human-Robot Interaction: Innovations in natural language processing and machine learning that allow for intuitive communication and collaboration between healthcare professionals and AI systems.
  • Surgical Room AI: Integration of AI within the operation room to improve workflow efficiency, patient safety, and outcome predictability.

üì• Submission Guidelines

Format: Papers will be submitted electronically following Lecture Notes in MICCAI 2024 format (max. 8 pages + 2 pages of references). Submissions exceeding page limit will be rejected without review. Latex style files can be found from Springer, which also contains Word instructions. The file format for submissions is Adobe Portable Document Format (PDF). Other formats will not be accepted.

Double Blind Review: EARTH reviewing is double blind. Please review the Anonymity guidelines of MICCAI main conference, and confirm that the author field does not break anonymity.

Paper Submission: EARTH uses the CMT system for online submission.

Supplemental Material: Supplemental material submission is optional, following same deadline as the main paper. Contents of the supplemental material would be referred to appropriately in the paper, while reviewers are not obliged to read them.

Submission Originality: Submissions should be original, no paper of substantially similar content should be under peer review or has been accepted for a publication elsewhere (conference/journal, not including archived work).

Proceedings: The proceedings of EARTH 2024 will be published as part of the joint MICCAI Workshops proceedings with the Springer LNCS Series.

üîç Review Process

  • All papers will be reviewed following a double-blind review process with at least 3 reviewers per submission.
  • We follow the MICCAI 2024 guideline regarding arXiv: Reviewers are strongly discouraged to search arXiv for submissions they are responsible to review. Even if they come across this information accidentally, they are discouraged to use the information in formulating their informed review of submissions. arXiv papers are not considered prior work since they have not been peer-reviewed. Therefore, citations to those papers are not required and reviewers are asked to not penalize a paper that fails to cite an arXiv submission.
  • Each reviewer will be able to cast a score from 1 (lowest) to 5 (highest) and papers with average scores higher than 2.5 will be considered acceptable.
  • The final decision about acceptance/rejection will be made by the PC member according to ranking, quality and the total number of submissions.
  • Outstanding papers will be selected for an oral presentation.
